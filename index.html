What is development?

Core concepts and key vocabulary for 16.1

Development is the progress a country has made in terms of economic growth, use of technology, and human welfare. It is influenced by economic, environmental, social, and political factors.

The development gap is the difference in standards of living between the world's richest and poorest countries.

Factors affecting development

Environmental
Natural hazards, climate, access to resources
Economic
Trade, debt, foreign investment
Social
Education, healthcare, access to safe water
Political
Stable government, civil war, corruption

Country classifications

HICs
GNI per capita above $12,535 — UK, most of Europe, North America, Australia.
LICs
GNI per capita below $1,036 — most are in Africa.
NEEs
Newly Emerging Economies — BRICS & MINT countries experiencing rapid industrial growth.
Exam tip: BRICS = Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa. MINT = Mexico, Indonesia, Nigeria, Turkey. Learn both acronyms — they appear regularly in exam questions.

Measuring development

HDICombines life expectancy, years of education, and GNI per head into a score 0–1Broader view — reflects social as well as economic factorsStill an average; does not measure happiness, freedom, or women's rights
Practice question (6 marks): "The HDI is the most effective measurement of development." Do you agree? Remember to define development, use evidence, and consider both sides.

Why HDI is widely used

HDI is popular because it gives a more complete picture than GNI alone. By including life expectancy and education, it shows whether economic growth is actually improving people's lives — not just increasing national income. It allows countries to be ranked on a scale of 0 to 1, making comparisons straightforward.

However, even HDI cannot measure everything. Quality of life also depends on safety, political freedom, women's rights, and personal happiness — none of which appear in any single statistic.

Exam practice

Structured guidance for the 6-mark question from the textbook

"The HDI is the most effective measurement of development." Do you agree? (6 marks)

This is a judgement question — you must take a position and support it with evidence.

Answer framework

Step 1 — Define development (1 mark)

Development is the progress a country makes in terms of economic growth, technology, and human welfare.

Step 2 — Agree with evidence (2 marks)

HDI is effective because it combines economic (GNI) and social (life expectancy, education) factors. Norway's HDI of 0.957 vs Niger's 0.394 clearly illustrates the scale of the development gap.

Step 3 — Disagree / limitations (2 marks)

However, HDI cannot measure quality of life factors such as happiness, political freedom, safety, or gender equality. GNI figures within HDI are still averages and may disguise inequality within countries.

Step 4 — Overall judgement (1 mark)

Overall, HDI is one of the most useful composite measures available, but no single indicator can fully capture development — a range of measures gives a more accurate picture.

Exam tips: Always define development early in your answer. Use actual figures (HDI values, GNI thresholds). Consider both sides before giving your overall judgement.
